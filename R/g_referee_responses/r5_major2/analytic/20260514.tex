\section{Modified HHI Measure}

We use a modified Herfindahl-type measure to capture the concentration of
trading intensity within a quarter. Let $d_t$ denote signed demand during
subperiod $t$ of a given quarter, and define cumulative signed demand in the
quarter as
\begin{equation}
    D = \sum_t d_t .
\end{equation}
If all demand realizations have the same sign, one can define demand shares as
\begin{equation}
    w_t = \frac{|d_t|}{\sum_\tau |d_\tau|},
\end{equation}
and compute a generalized concentration measure
\begin{equation}
    H(\beta)
    =
    \left( \sum_t w_t^\beta \right)^{1/(\beta-1)},
    \qquad \beta>1.
    \label{eq:hhi}
\end{equation}
When $\beta=2$, this measure coincides with the standard Herfindahl-Hirschman
Index: $H(2)=\sum_t w_t^2$. In the empirical analysis, we primarily use $\beta=2$, but we also consider alternative values of $\beta$ to examine robustness.

In the data, signed demand need not have the same sign throughout the quarter.
Because our goal is to measure the concentration of trading pressure in the
quarter's main direction, we first identify the sign of net quarterly demand,
$\operatorname{sign}(D)$. For quarters with $D\neq 0$, define same-direction
demand as
\begin{equation}
    \tilde d_t
    =
    d_t \cdot
    \mathbf{1}\left\{
    \operatorname{sign}(d_t)=\operatorname{sign}(D)
    \right\}.
\end{equation}
Let
\begin{equation}
    \tilde G
    =
    \sum_t |\tilde d_t|
\end{equation}
denote total same-direction gross demand. We then define same-direction demand
shares as
\begin{equation}
    \tilde w_t
    =
    \frac{|\tilde d_t|}{\tilde G},
\end{equation}
and compute the modified concentration measure as
\begin{equation}
    \tilde H(\beta)
    =
    \left( \sum_t \tilde w_t^\beta \right)^{1/(\beta-1)}.
    \label{eq:modified_hhi}
\end{equation}
Thus, $\tilde H(\beta)$ captures how concentrated demand is among the subperiods that contribute to the quarter's net direction.
